The AI ROI framework — memorise this
1
Identify the process

High-volume, repetitive, clear inputs/outputs — best candidates for AI

2
Quantify current cost

Hours per task × volume × cost per hour = baseline annual cost

3
Estimate AI impact

% reduction in time or FTEs × baseline = annual saving

4
Subtract AI cost

API cost + implementation + maintenance = total AI cost

5
Calculate payback period

Net saving ÷ upfront investment = months to break even

DAY 05 AI Ecosystem — Models, Tools, Tokenisation & Cost of AI Strategy ▼
What to do today
  • Learn the model landscape and what each is best known for (see concept box below)
  • Understand tokenisation & cost of AI: you pay per token (input + output). 1000 tokens ≈ 750 words. GPT-4o costs ~$5/million input tokens. For a business, this means: high-volume API calls add up — you need to estimate cost at scale before committing.
  • Know cost minimisation strategies: use smaller/cheaper models for simple tasks · cache common queries · use prompt compression · choose the right model tier (flash/haiku for speed, opus/4o for complexity)
  • Understand the 3 AI strategy options: Build (custom, expensive, differentiated) · Buy SaaS (fast, generic) · API/Partner (flexible, middle ground)
  • Visit artificialanalysis.ai — understand the model comparison leaderboard
Model landscape — what each is best known for

OpenAI GPT-4o / o3 — most widely used, best coding, powers ChatGPT. o3 is best for complex reasoning tasks.
Anthropic Claude 3.5/3.7 — best for long documents, nuanced writing, safety-critical applications. Most used in enterprise.
Google Gemini 1.5/2.0 — best multimodal (text + images + video + audio). Deepest Google Workspace integration.
Meta Llama 3.3 — open source, free to run on your own servers. Best for sensitive data (nothing leaves your infrastructure).
Mistral Large — European open source, fast and efficient. Good for cost-sensitive deployments.

As a business owner: don't be loyal to one model. Use the right model for the right task. Claude for docs/analysis, GPT-4o for coding/general, Gemini for multimodal, Llama for on-premise sensitive data.

Build vs Buy vs Partner — decision matrix

Build when: core to competitive advantage · unique proprietary data · long-term differentiation needed · have technical team · no off-shelf solution exists

Buy (SaaS) when: commodity function · need speed to market · limited tech resources · standard use case (e.g. Salesforce Einstein for CRM AI, Zendesk AI for support)

API/Partner when: need AI capabilities but not AI infrastructure · want flexibility to switch models · moderate customisation needed (e.g. call OpenAI/Anthropic API and build your own UI on top)

Vendor evaluation questions to ask: How is my data used? Can I switch models? What's the uptime SLA? What happens to my data if I leave? Is it compliant with UAE PDPL?

DAY 11 AI Governance, Ethics, Bias & Future Trends (incl. AGI) Governance ▼
What to do today
  • AI Bias & Fairness (your notes: "banking not leaning to AI, credit system, housewife"): Understand why this is a real issue — AI trained on historical data can perpetuate discrimination. Example: a credit model trained on past approvals may systematically reject homemakers or certain demographics because they were underrepresented in historical data. Regulators in EU/UAE now require explainability.
  • Explainable AI (XAI): Being able to explain why the AI made a decision — critical in lending, insurance, healthcare. "The model rejected this application because income-to-debt ratio was 0.8 and employment tenure was under 6 months" vs a black box answer.
  • UAE PDPL: Know what personal data you can process with AI, consent requirements, and cross-border transfer rules
  • Future trends — AGI & what's next: AGI = Artificial General Intelligence — AI that matches human-level reasoning across all tasks. Not here yet. Timeline is debated: OpenAI believes 2025–2030, most academics say much longer. Know: near-term trends (agentic AI, multimodal, browser-use agents) vs long-term (AGI, AI in physical world / robotics)
AI bias in credit/banking — the key example to know

The problem: A bank trains an AI credit model on 10 years of approved loans. Historically, homemakers (often women) and recent immigrants were rarely approved. The AI learns this pattern and continues rejecting them — not because they're bad borrowers, but because of historical bias in the training data.

Why banks are cautious about AI: Regulatory risk (cannot explain AI decisions to regulators), reputational risk (discrimination lawsuits), and reliability risk (model drift over time).

The solution: Bias audits, diverse training data, XAI layers, human review for borderline cases, continuous monitoring.

Future trends to know — near-term and long-term

Now (2025): Agentic AI is the dominant trend — AI that acts, not just answers · MCP standard for tool connectivity · Reasoning models (o3, Claude 3.7) that think step-by-step · Browser-use agents that operate websites like humans

Next 3 years: AI in physical world (humanoid robots — Figure, Tesla Optimus) · Multimodal agents seeing/hearing/acting · AI colleagues that work autonomously for hours · Universal AI assistants embedded in all software

Long-term (AGI): AI with general reasoning across all domains. Not here yet. Biggest debate in tech: OpenAI/Anthropic racing toward it, many researchers say it's decades away. As a business owner: don't bet on AGI, bet on the practical agentic tools available today.
